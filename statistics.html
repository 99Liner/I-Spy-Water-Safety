Game Statistics

Our interactive game is designed not just for fun, but also for learning. The statistics below demonstrate the effectiveness of the game in teaching players about lake water safety. Each dot represents a player's performance, with the number of scenarios correctly handled on the vertical axis and the time taken to complete the game on the horizontal axis.

First Playthrough

In the initial round of gameplay, players are often getting acquainted with the game mechanics and lake safety rules. The scatter in the "First Playthrough" graph represents the learning curve as players begin to understand the importance of making safe choices. While the spread is broad, indicating varying levels of initial knowledge, there's a general trend that suggests players improve as they continue playing.

Second Playthrough

The "Second Playthrough" graph shows a noticeable shift towards higher scores in less time, indicating that players are learning from their first experience and applying their knowledge more effectively. This improvement underscores the educational value of the game - with repeated play, users are not only reinforcing their knowledge but also their ability to react quickly and correctly in water safety scenarios.

These insights aren't just numbers; they're a testament to our commitment to combining education with engagement. By tracking these statistics, we can continuously refine our game to ensure it remains an effective learning tool for lake water safety.
